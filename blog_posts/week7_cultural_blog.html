Affirmation and Stereotype ThreatSeptember 19, 2014

When exploring my values at a time when I was the happiest, proudest and most satisfied, those values that stand out in my mind are:

accomplishment | accountability | Achievement | Adventure | Affection | Ambition | Close relationships | Commitment | Compassion | Confidence | Cooperation | Courage | Credibility | Enthusiasm | Excellence | Excitement | Fairness | Friendships | Helping other people | Influence | Inspiration | Integrity | Leadership | Loyalty | Meaningful work | Mentorship | Money | Nature | Peace | Personal development | Physical challenge | Pleasure | Quality | Reputation | Responsibility | Self-respect | Stability | Truth | Will-power | Wisdom.

To pick just one value that is 'most' important to me is really kind of hard. I have a few of these that are at the top and are all equal. Close relationships, commitment, compassion, credibility, enthusiasm, excitement, integrity, just to name a few. Lets take enthusiasm. I think that everything starts within. One must be happy, generally, if you think everything is going to suck, then it most likely will suck! If your not happy people don't want to hang out with you. If you are not happy, people don't want to work with you. For me it is really important that I know that I am happy, that I am enthusiastic, I think others that are around me feed off that, makes them happy and feel good. I find a lot more gets accomplished when everyone is enthusiastic.


From 1 to 5 I live up to these values:

  • Close relationships 4
  • Commitment 5
  • Compassion 5
  • Credibility 5
  • Enthusiasm 5
  • Excellence 4
  • Excitement 5
  • Integrity 4

The topic that someone asked my advice on was basically money. This person has a lot of debt and asked me what my thoughts were on pulling retirement funds to pay debt off!


Makes me really happy when I think about my core values. Makes me excited when I live my life with in my values. I am not sure if values can help me mediate stereotype threat. But I think I need to learn how! I'm a pretty positive and happy person. That doesn't mean I don't have my bad days and when I do, I will try and ask for help! As a scuba diver, and scuba diver instructor, the one thing we teach and I now have used in my everyday life, when you feel a stressful moment coming on, do three things, stop, breath, and think! Over reaction is a killer!
